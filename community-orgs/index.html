Introduction to Community Organization

Community organization is a process by which a community identifies its needs or objectives, orders these needs or objectives, develops the confidence and will to work at these needs or objectives, finds the resources to deal with these needs or objectives, takes action in respect to them, and in so doing extends and develops cooperative and collaborative attitudes and practices in the community.

Community organization is both a planned process to accomplish community objectives and an active participation by the community residents in a program of improvements.

Expert Definitions

Murray G. Ross

"A process by which a community identifies its needs or objectives, orders these needs or objectives, develops the confidence and will to work at these needs or objectives."

Arthur Dunham

"A conscious process of social work concerned with adjusting the relationship between social welfare resources and social welfare needs within a geographical area or functional field."

Walter W. Pettit

"The art and process of developing active and healthy relationships among people and social welfare organizations in any area."

Community Organization Settings

Rural Settings

Characteristics

  • Agricultural focus
  • Traditional values
  • Close-knit communities
  • Limited resources

Challenges

  • Infrastructure gaps
  • Resource scarcity
  • Limited access to services
  • Migration issues

Rural Development

  • Sustainable agriculture
  • Skill development
  • Resource management
  • Community empowerment

Urban Settings

Characteristics

  • Dense population
  • Diverse communities
  • Modern infrastructure
  • Complex social systems

Challenges

  • Housing issues
  • Social inequality
  • Environmental concerns
  • Community fragmentation

Urban Development

  • Social integration
  • Economic opportunities
  • Environmental sustainability
  • Cultural preservation
