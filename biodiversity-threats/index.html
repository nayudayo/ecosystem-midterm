Introduction to Biodiversity

Biodiversity refers to the variety of living species on Earth, including plants, animals, bacteria, and fungi. While Earth's biodiversity is so rich that many species have yet to be discovered, many species are being threatened.

Over 150,300 species are on the IUCN Red List. 42,100 of these are in danger of extinction in the wild.

Source: IUCN Red List - a critical indicator of the health of the world's biodiversity.

Major Threats to Biodiversity

Current Status

Global wildlife populations have dropped by an alarming 69% on average since 1970. Some experts believe we're witnessing the sixth mass extinction event in Earth's history.

Habitat Destruction

Primary cause of species extinction worldwide.

  • Agriculture expansion
  • Urban development
  • Resource extraction
  • Deforestation

Habitat Fragmentation

Breaking up continuous habitats into smaller, isolated parts.

  • Decreased population size
  • Increased disease susceptibility
  • Reduced adaptability
  • Higher mortality rates

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and altered weather patterns affect ecosystems.

  • Temperature changes
  • Altered precipitation
  • Sea level rise
  • Ocean acidification

Disease

691 species are threatened by disease.

  • 548 amphibians affected
  • 95 bird species
  • 48 mammal species
  • Increased pathogen spread

Pollution Types

Soil Pollution

  • Industrial waste
  • Chemical contamination
  • Excessive fertilizers

Air Pollution

  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial emissions
  • Particulate matter

Water Pollution

  • Industrial effluents
  • Marine dumping
  • Oil spills

Overexploitation

72% of studied species (6,241) are threatened by hunting, fishing, or logging.

  • Industrialized fishing
  • Illegal wildlife trade
  • Deforestation
  • Excessive harvesting
