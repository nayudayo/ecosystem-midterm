Introduction

The Earth is home to millions of species, including over 8.2 billion humans. While humans are creative and intelligent beings, our impact on the planet can also be destructive.

Key Points

  • We share the Earth with millions of species
  • Current human population exceeds 8.2 billion
  • Human activities significantly impact the environment

Consequences of Overpopulation

Famine

Food shortages increase as demand rises.

  • Increased food demand
  • Agricultural pressure
  • Resource competition

Poverty

Resources become scarcer, increasing financial hardship.

  • Resource scarcity
  • Economic pressure
  • Income inequality

War

Competition for resources can lead to conflict.

  • Resource conflicts
  • Territorial disputes
  • Social tensions

Disease

High population density leads to faster disease spread.

  • Rapid transmission
  • Healthcare strain
  • Sanitation challenges

Environmental Damage

Deforestation, pollution, and habitat loss.

  • Habitat destruction
  • Air and water pollution
  • Climate change impact

Species Loss

Wildlife suffers due to human expansion.

  • Biodiversity loss
  • Ecosystem disruption
  • Extinction risks

Population Growth Trends

Key Observations

  • Growth rate is slowly declining (from 1.36% in 2000 to 0.87% in 2024)
  • Population density continues to increase
  • Net population change remains significant

Growth Rate Trends

While the yearly growth rate is declining, the absolute number of people added each year remains high due to the larger base population.
