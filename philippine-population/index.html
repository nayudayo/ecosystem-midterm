Overview

The Philippines, a captivating archipelago comprised of over 7,600 islands, is located in Southeast Asia in the western Pacific Ocean. This strategic location has contributed to its rich and diverse cultural heritage.

Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)

Primary source of Philippine population data through:

  • Census of Population and Housing (CPH)
  • Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS)
  • Civil Registration System
  • Population Projections

Key Population Characteristics

Population Size and Growth

As of January 2024:

  • Total population: 118.2 million
  • Growth rate: +1.5 percent (2023-2024)
  • Increase: 1.8 million in one year

Core Factors Affecting Population Growth

Birth Rate (Fertility)

The number of births within a population during a specific period. It's a key factor in how quickly a population grows.

Death Rate (Mortality)

The number of deaths within a population during a specific period. It counteracts the birth rate, and changes in it significantly impact population growth.

Migration

Movement of people across geographical boundaries:

  • Immigration: People moving into an area
  • Emigration: People moving out of an area

Demographics

Age Structure

The Philippines is characterized by a relatively young population, with a significant portion below 25 years old.

Geographic Distribution

As of 2024:

  • Urban population: 48.7% (56,434,179 people)
  • Growing trend towards urbanization
  • Metro Manila as major urban center

Religious Demographics

Religious Distribution (2020)

  • Roman Catholicism: 78.8%
  • Islam: 6.4%
  • Iglesia ni Cristo: 2.6%
  • Philippine Independent Church: 1.4%
  • Other Christians: 1.9%
  • Other / None: 8.9%

Gender Demographics

Sex Ratio (2020 Census)

  • 103 males per 100 females
  • More males in younger age groups
  • More females in older age groups

Migration Patterns

Internal Migration

Movement patterns within the Philippines:

  • Rural to urban migration
  • Focus on Metro Manila
  • Driven by employment and education

External Migration

Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs):

  • Major destinations: Saudi Arabia, UAE
  • Significant economic impact through remittances
  • Global presence in Asia, Europe, and North America

Cultural Diversity

Ethnic Groups

  • Tagalog
  • Visayan (Cebuano, Hiligaynon)
  • Ilocano
  • Bicolano
  • Indigenous groups (Igorot, Lumad)

Linguistic Diversity

  • Filipino (national language)
  • English (widely used)
  • Regional languages
  • Local dialects

Health Characteristics

Major Health Challenges

  • Communicable diseases (TB, dengue, HIV/AIDS)
  • Non-communicable diseases
  • Vector-borne diseases
  • Maternal and child health issues

Healthcare Access

  • Urban-rural disparities
  • PhilHealth coverage gaps
  • Quality of service variations
  • Decentralized health services

Infant Mortality (2022)

  • 19.8 deaths per 1,000 live births
  • Under-5 mortality: 26.5 per 1,000 births
  • Affected by prenatal care access
  • Regional variations exist

Socioeconomic Characteristics

Poverty & Income

  • Regional poverty variations
  • Income inequality challenges
  • Urban-rural disparities

Education & Literacy

  • High literacy rates
  • Variable education quality
  • Rural access challenges

Employment

  • Unemployment challenges
  • Significant OFW contribution
  • Informal sector prevalence
